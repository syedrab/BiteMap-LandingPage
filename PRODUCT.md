# BiteMap — Product Context

## Product Purpose
BiteMap is a restaurant discovery app that surfaces video reviews from local food creators. It's the anti-Yelp: no star ratings from strangers, no fake reviews — just TikTok-style video content from real food people in your city. The landing page's one job is converting cold social traffic (Instagram ads) into app downloads.

## Register
brand

## Users
**Primary:** Food-curious urbanites 20–35 in Toronto, Vancouver, NYC, LA. They're already on Instagram. They don't trust Yelp. They've found restaurants through TikTok. They want to eat somewhere good tonight and they're on their phone.

**Secondary:** Food creators who want their content surfaced to hungry locals.

## Brand
- **Name:** BiTEMaP (mixed case — intentional)
- **Tone:** Direct, local, a little editorial. Not corporate. Not food-blogger gushing. Confident and specific.
- **Anti-references:** Generic app store screenshot pages. Yelp/Google Maps. "Discover amazing restaurants" filler copy. SaaS hero templates with three feature cards.
- **Personality:** The friend who always knows the best spot. Opinionated. City-native.

## Design Tokens
- **Primary accent:** #FF6B35 (orange)
- **Background:** #ffffff
- **Text:** #1a1a1a
- **Secondary text:** #666
- **Fonts:** Baloo 2 (headings/logo), Space Grotesk (body)
- **Border radius:** 12–46px (generous, app-like)

## Strategic Principles
1. Instagram-first traffic — assume mobile, assume cold, assume 3-second attention window
2. The phone mockup IS the proof — show the actual app experience
3. Specificity beats claims — "312 videos in Toronto" beats "thousands of reviews"
4. One CTA: download the app. Everything else is distraction.
