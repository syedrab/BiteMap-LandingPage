Toronto's Halal Food Scene

Toronto is home to one of the most diverse halal food scenes in North America. The city's multicultural fabric means halal options span far beyond the typical shawarma shop. From Scarborough's legendary biryani spots along Lawrence Avenue to the bustling downtown shawarma joints on Yonge Street, the Thorncliffe Park samosa and chaat scene, and Mississauga's thriving Pakistani restaurant corridor along Dixie Road — the Greater Toronto Area has incredible halal options across every cuisine imaginable.

Middle Eastern, South Asian, Turkish, Somali, Malaysian, Afghan, and even halal Japanese and Korean — Toronto's halal restaurant landscape reflects the communities that call this city home. Whether you are a lifelong Torontonian or visiting for the first time, the depth and quality of halal dining here will surprise you. BiteMap's food creators are out there every week filming at these restaurants so you can discover spots you would never find on your own.

Popular Halal Cuisines on BiteMap

Shawarma & Middle Eastern

From classic chicken shawarma wraps to loaded platters with garlic sauce, hummus, and tabouleh. Toronto's Middle Eastern halal restaurants serve some of the best shawarma outside the Levant.

Biryani & South Asian

Hyderabadi biryani, nihari, karahi, butter chicken, and dosa. Scarborough, Thorncliffe Park, and Mississauga are packed with South Asian halal restaurants serving authentic regional dishes.

Kebabs & Turkish/Afghan

Juicy lamb and chicken kebabs, pide, lahmacun, and mantu. Turkish and Afghan restaurants across Toronto serve grilled meats and fresh bread that are hard to beat.

Halal Burgers & Fried Chicken

Smashed burgers, Nashville hot chicken, loaded fries, and milkshakes — all halal. Toronto's halal fast-casual scene has exploded with creative, high-quality options.

Halal Fine Dining

Upscale halal restaurants offering prix fixe menus, wagyu steaks, and refined Middle Eastern and South Asian cuisine for special occasions and date nights.

Halal Desserts & Bakeries

Kunafa, baklava, gulab jamun, Turkish ice cream, and halal-certified cakes. Toronto's halal bakeries and dessert shops are destinations in their own right.
