Explore Toronto Neighborhoods

Part of what makes Toronto's food scene special is how each neighborhood brings its own character. The restaurants in Kensington Market feel nothing like the restaurants in Yorkville, even though they are a 15-minute walk apart. BiteMap lets you explore restaurants by neighborhood, so you can find the best spots wherever you are in the city.

Downtown Core Kensington Market Queen West The Danforth Little Italy Chinatown Liberty Village Leslieville Yorkville

Kensington Market is home to eclectic international eats packed into narrow streets. Queen West mixes trendy cocktail bars with late-night eats. The Danforth remains the heart of Toronto's Greek food scene but has expanded to include everything from ramen to dim sum. Chinatown stretches along Spadina with some of the best and most affordable meals in the city. Little Italy on College Street delivers far more than pasta. Liberty Village draws a younger crowd with modern brunch spots and craft breweries. Leslieville on Queen East has quietly become one of the best food neighborhoods in the city. And Yorkville offers upscale dining for special occasions.

Open the BiteMap map and zoom into any neighborhood. Every pin is a restaurant with at least one video review from a trusted food creator.
