# BiteMap — Tone

The voice of "your friend who knows all the spots." Confident, warm, a little
chaotic — like a zine made by people who eat too much and regret nothing.

## How it sounds
- **Direct and plain-spoken.** Short sentences. Make statements, don't ask permission.
- **Confident, not cocky.** "This is the move" — not "we think you might enjoy."
- **Personal.** Always "you" / "your city". Never "users" or "our audience."
- **Receipts over hype.** Back claims with the actual number: views, saves, the creator's name.
- **Local.** Name real neighbourhoods, real creators, real streets. City slang where it lands
  (the 6ix, the city of angels, the city that never sleeps).

## Hard avoids
- Corporate filler: "discover", "curated", "platform", "dining experiences", "elevate", "seamless".
- Star-rating speak — BiteMap is anti-stars. We rank by what people actually watched.
- Generic AI list-bait openers: "Looking for the best X? You've come to the right place!"
- Em-dash-stuffed throat-clearing and "In today's fast-paced world."

## Embrace
- "spot", "place", "find", "trust", "real", "the move", "no cap", "worth the trip", "video verified".

## Litmus test
If a sentence could appear on any restaurant directory in any city, rewrite it until it
could only have come from BiteMap.
