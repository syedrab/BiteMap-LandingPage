Pho Ngoc Yen
◉ TIKTOK 2:14 MUST TRY
2.4M 188K 22K
Keith Lee
17.2M · TikTok
"The broth is the cleanest I've had outside of Vietnam. 9.3/10. Driving back tomorrow.
Caption
I've been looking for legit pho in Toronto for MONTHS. Finally. Pho Ngoc Yen on Dundas — the broth is clear, deep, and you can taste every hour it simmered. Rare beef, tendon, brisket. $14. I'm not okay.
Alright y'all so I just sat down at Pho Ngoc Yen. This place has been on my list since I landed in Toronto. Let me tell you about this broth first — it's clear, like you can see through it, but the flavor? Deep. Like 12-hour simmer deep. The rare beef they slice it paper thin and it cooks right in the bowl. Now the tendon — perfectly gelatinous, not chewy. The brisket has this fat cap that just melts. And the herbs — fresh Thai basil, bean sprouts, lime, hoisin on the side. I'm going 9.3 out of 10. I'll be back tomorrow. Not even joking.
9.3/10
Pho Tien Thanh
▶ YOUTUBE 8:42 GOAT
1.1M 94K 12K
Mikey Chen
9.8M · YouTube
"The bowl I've been chasing for ten years. Fat cap on the brisket?? Forget it.
Caption
Pho Tien Thanh on Ossington. Cash only. No frills. Just the best pho in Toronto. The brisket has a fat cap that renders in the broth. I've been doing this for 15 years and this is top 5 worldwide.
So Pho Tien Thanh — this is the spot everyone whispers about but nobody talks about on camera. Cash only, no BS. You walk in and it smells like someone's been making broth since 4 AM because they have. I ordered the large special with everything — rare beef, brisket, tendon, tripe. That fat cap on the brisket? It renders right into the broth and creates this silky layer on top. The noodles are perfectly cooked, not mushy. Bean sprouts are fresh. I've had pho in Saigon, in LA, in Houston — this stands with all of them. Top 5 bowl worldwide for me. I'm dead serious.
★★★★★

Caption
Pho Hu'ng Chinatown — my go-to weekday lunch spot. Fast, clean, and the rare beef is sliced so thin it cooks in seconds. Under $12 for a large. This is the answer.
Ok quick lunch review — Pho Hu'ng on Spadina. You know this place, it's been here forever. The move is the rare beef pho, large. They slice the beef so thin you can almost see through it and it cooks perfectly in the broth. The broth itself is solid — not the most complex I've had but exactly what you want at noon on a Tuesday. Clean, beefy, hits the spot. Under twelve bucks for a large with all the fixings. I come here at least once a week. Not trying to reinvent the wheel, just really good pho.

Anh & Chi
▶ YOUTUBE 11:08 MUST TRY
2.1M 140K 18K
Chef Anh Nguyen
2.1M · YouTube
"My grandmother would approve. And she approves of nothing. Star anise hits at the right moment.
Caption
Anh & Chi on the Danforth. This is elevated Vietnamese done right. The pho has layers — star anise, cinnamon, charred ginger. $$$ but worth every cent for a special meal.
Anh and Chi on the Danforth — this is not your typical pho spot. This is elevated Vietnamese cuisine and the pho reflects that. The broth is built in layers. You get the star anise upfront, then the cinnamon comes through mid-sip, and then there's this charred ginger note at the finish. The beef is premium cuts, beautifully sliced. They serve it with these handmade condiments that are next level. Yes it's more expensive than your average bowl but this is a dining experience. My grandmother — who is Vietnamese — tried it and said it reminded her of home. She approves of nothing. That's the highest praise I can give.
GRANDMA ✓

Pho Metro in Scarborough. Yes I drove 45 minutes. Yes I'd do it again. This is old-school pho made by someone who's been doing it for decades. The real deal.
Listen I don't usually drive to Scarborough for food but someone in my DMs would not stop telling me about Pho Metro. So I went. Forty-five minutes in traffic. And you know what? They were right. This tastes like someone's Vietnamese mom has been perfecting this recipe since the seventies. The broth has that old-school depth you can't fake — it's probably been going since dawn. The portions are massive. The price is nothing. I'm annoyed I didn't know about this sooner. Would I drive ninety minutes? Honestly? Yeah. Yeah I would.

Alright so everyone told me to try this place and I really wanted to love it. I went at peak lunch, sat down, ordered the special. The noodles came out soft — like overcooked soft. The broth was thin compared to other spots I've been to this week. The rare beef was fine but nothing special. Look, maybe I caught them on an off day. Maybe the hype set my expectations too high. But based on this visit? It's a 6 out of 10 for me. I'll try again because I believe in second chances but right now? Mid.
